# Old-regime deductions catalogue

These deductions are available **only in the old regime** (with a couple of
exceptions noted). They are what can make the old regime cheaper, so if the user
is considering it, collect the proofs and total them up. Re-confirm current-year
limits — they change.

> Quick exceptions that survive in the **new** regime too: employer's NPS
> contribution **80CCD(2)**, and a few niche items. Everything else below is
> old-regime only.

## The big ones

- **Standard deduction (salary):** ₹50,000 (old) — automatic, no proof needed.
- **80C / 80CCC / 80CCD(1):** combined cap **₹1,50,000**. Includes EPF, PPF, ELSS,
  life-insurance premium, principal repayment of home loan, children's tuition
  fees, 5-yr tax-saver FD, NSC, Sukanya Samriddhi, etc.
- **80CCD(1B):** extra **₹50,000** for NPS, over and above the 80C cap.
- **80CCD(2):** employer's NPS contribution — up to 10% of salary (14% for govt /
  for new-regime where allowed). This one works in **both** regimes.
- **80D (health insurance):** self+family **₹25,000** (**₹50,000** if the insured
  is a senior citizen); **plus** parents **₹25,000** (**₹50,000** if senior).
  Preventive health check-up ₹5,000 within these limits.
- **Home-loan interest (Section 24(b)):** up to **₹2,00,000** for a self-occupied
  house; no cap for a let-out house (set-off limited to ₹2L against other heads,
  rest carried forward). Old regime only for self-occupied.
- **HRA (Section 10(13A)):** exempt = least of (actual HRA; rent − 10% of salary;
  50% of salary in metro / 40% non-metro). Needs rent receipts / landlord PAN if
  rent > ₹1L/yr. Old regime only.

## Others worth checking

- **80TTA:** savings-bank interest up to **₹10,000** (non-seniors). **80TTB:**
  ₹50,000 of interest for senior citizens. Old regime only.
- **80E:** full interest on an education loan (no cap), up to 8 years.
- **80G:** donations — 50% or 100% deductible depending on the institution; some
  subject to a 10%-of-income qualifying limit. Cash donations > ₹2,000 don't
  qualify. Need the donee's PAN and an 80G receipt.
- **80EEB:** interest on an electric-vehicle loan, up to ₹1,50,000.
- **80DD / 80DDB / 80U:** disability / specified-disease deductions.
- **80GG:** rent paid when no HRA is received.
- **LTA (Section 10(5)):** leave travel allowance exemption for domestic travel,
  twice in a block of four years.

## How to use this in the comparison

For the old-regime figure: taxable income = gross income − ₹50,000 std deduction −
(80C up to 1.5L) − (80CCD(1B) up to 50k) − 80D − home-loan interest − HRA exemption
− any others, floored at zero. Then apply the old-regime slabs. Compare that total
tax (with cess) against the new-regime total. **Only count deductions the user
actually has and can prove** — don't inflate the old-regime case with aspirational
investments. If the user is unsure what they have, list the categories and ask.
