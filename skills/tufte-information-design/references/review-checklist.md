# Review Checklist

Audit any information display — slide, document, dashboard, artifact, table, diagram, or email — before finalizing it, or when asked to critique one.
Grouped by the seven principles; finish with the three closing tests and the medium addendum.

## Universal checklist

### 1. Show the evidence

- [ ] Every element loses information if removed (data-ink audit).
- [ ] No redundant carriers: labeled data has no legend; labeled bars need no dense axis.
- [ ] The display leads with data, not with structure or branding.

### 2. Tell the truth

- [ ] Visual proportions match data proportions (Lie Factor ≈ 1.0).
- [ ] Baselines are honest — zero for bars/areas; truncated axes are flagged when used.
- [ ] Scales are consistent across everything being compared.
- [ ] One data dimension maps to one visual dimension — no area/volume for 1-D values, no 3-D.
- [ ] Full relevant range shown; the time window isn't cherry-picked.
- [ ] Sources, units, and sample sizes are stated on the display.

### 3. Erase what doesn't inform

- [ ] No 3-D effects, gradients, shadows, bevels, or decorative imagery.
- [ ] Gridlines are light, sparse, behind the data — or gone.
- [ ] Borders and boxes removed where alignment and spacing can group instead.
- [ ] Fills are solid and muted; no moiré patterns.

### 4. Layer and separate

- [ ] The loudest ink is the most important information.
- [ ] Structure (axes, grids, connectors, chrome) is muted; content carries the intensity.
- [ ] Color is a small, intense signal on a quiet ground — not large saturated fields.
- [ ] Negative space is doing the grouping work.

### 5. Enable comparison

- [ ] The relevant "compared to what?" is on the display, not left to memory.
- [ ] Comparable panels share design, scale, and ordering (small-multiples discipline).
- [ ] Parallel things are presented in parallel form — same units, order, phrasing.

### 6. Integrate evidence with narrative

- [ ] Data is labeled directly; notable points are annotated on the display.
- [ ] Exhibits sit at the point of the claim they support — no "see figure 3" round trips.
- [ ] Each exhibit stands alone: finding in the title/caption, units on the axes, source cited.

### 7. Respect the audience

- [ ] Titles state findings, not topics.
- [ ] Revision raised content resolution rather than deleting content to look clean.
- [ ] Complexity that matters is shown and layered, not hidden.

## Closing tests

- **Squint test:** blur your eyes or step back — the main message and hierarchy must survive.
- **Newspaper test:** could this appear in a quality publication and be understood without you presenting it?
- **Necessity test:** the display itself, and each element on it, would be missed if cut.

## Medium addenda

### Slides

- [ ] One claim on this slide; title states it.
- [ ] No nested bullets; fragments upgraded to sentences, tables, or a diagram.
- [ ] Template decoration (logos, dividers, backgrounds) stripped.

### Documents & posts

- [ ] First paragraph carries the claim.
- [ ] Figures adjacent to the sentences they support.
- [ ] Three-plus compared numbers are in a table, not prose.

### Dashboards & artifacts

- [ ] Every stat tile has a comparison (prior, target, or trend).
- [ ] Comparable panels share scales.
- [ ] Chrome muted; one accent color for state.

### Tables

- [ ] Horizontal rules only; numbers right-aligned; text left-aligned.
- [ ] Units in headers; rows ordered by meaning.

### Diagrams

- [ ] One relationship type; edges labeled.
- [ ] Flow direction consistent; boxes muted, flow emphasized.

## Quick fixes

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Cluttered chart | Delete gridlines, direct-label, mute the frame |
| Legend decoding | Label data directly |
| "Looks boring" | Add comparison and context, not decoration |
| Wall of text | Convert to table (comparison) or diagram (structure) |
| Wall of bullets | Rewrite as sentences stating relationships |
| Distorted impression | Check Lie Factor, baselines, scales |
| Too many colors | One muted ground + one intense signal |
| Vague title | Rewrite as the finding |
| Number without meaning | Attach "compared to what" |
